Why switching wins twice as often

Your first pick has a 1/3 chance of being right. That means there's a 2/3 chance the prize is behind one of the other two doors. The host knows where the prize is and always opens an empty door from those two — which concentrates that 2/3 probability onto the single remaining unopened door.

So staying wins 1/3 of the time. Switching wins 2/3 of the time. Run a few hundred sims above and you'll watch the percentages converge.

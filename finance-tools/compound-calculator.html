üí° Understanding Compound Interest

The Compound Effect: Unlike simple interest, compound interest earns returns on both your original investment AND previous earnings, creating exponential growth over time.
Time is Your Friend: Starting early, even with small amounts, often beats starting late with larger amounts due to the power of compounding.
Frequency Matters: More frequent compounding (daily vs. annually) can significantly boost returns over long periods.
Consistency Wins: Regular contributions, even small ones, can dramatically increase your final balance through dollar-cost averaging.

üéØ Investment Tips

Start Early: Time is the most powerful factor in compound growth. Even $25/month starting at age 25 can outperform $100/month starting at age 35.
Automate Contributions: Set up automatic transfers to take advantage of dollar-cost averaging and ensure consistent investing.
Increase Contributions: Boost your contributions with raises and windfalls to accelerate wealth building.
Stay Invested: Avoid withdrawing funds early. Let compound interest work its magic over the full time horizon.
Consider Tax Advantages: Use 401(k), IRA, and other tax-advantaged accounts to maximize your compound growth potential.
